Providing Context

To develop a comprehensive view of a student with special education needs in FSL programs, it is beneficial to discuss the unique learning profile and context of that student. This information could include the student's current grade and FSL program, current level of achievement, past experience in FSL programs, current classroom setting , accomodations required, student's interests, and helpful teaching strategies.

Student Strengths

An essential element in establishing a student's unique learning profile is the areas of strengths as they affect his or her ability to learn and demonstrate learning. These areas of strengths may include prefered learning styles/modalities, previously acquired learning skills (e.g. organizational skills, time management) and cognitive processing and communication.

Student Needs

In describing the student learning profile it is important to also consider the areas of needs which should make evident the reasons that he or she requires additional support. Areas of needs may refer to information regarding broad cognitive or processing challenge (e.g., in the area of visual memory) and/or skills deficit that interfere with the student's ability to learn (e.g., social skills, attention control, expressive language).

Considerations

When planning to support students with special education needs in FSL programs it is also benficial to condiser factors, aside from the student's strengths and needs, that could impact their learning in FSL. These factors may not necessarily be specific to the student but could still affect them. These may include existing practices or policies, modifications, pathways, research on best practices, and access to technology.

Strategies

A key element to include in planning conversations to support students with special education needs in FSL programs is the range of strategies to help the student in achieving his or her learning expectations based on his or her learning profile and needs. These strategies may involve instructional, assessment and/or intervention strategies. It is also important to state how and who will implement the strategies.

Next Steps

Planning conversations are an initial step in supporting students with special educations needs in FSL programs. It is important to identify a plan to move forward and consider the next steps. These steps may involve contacting other teachers and parents, and meeting regurlarly to review progress.
